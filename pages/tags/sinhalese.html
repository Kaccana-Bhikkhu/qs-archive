4. Comment by Randula Haththotuwa: Exploring the different qualities or expressions of Nibbāna, I was just comparing them with the Sinhalese language. I wonder whether there are similarities in Pāli. You said Nibindatti. Bindinava means breaking up in Sinhalese. When you say Nibbāna, if you just use the second half, bana. To give an example of what bana means, let’s say you have a truckload of things. Taking the things out, lowering them down and putting them down means bana. So the whole process is emptying out. I don’t know how it is in Pali. [Nibbāna] [Pāli] [Sinhalese] // [Etymology] [Ven. Ananda Maitreya]
